Nicholas K. DeWind


I'm a cognitive neuroscientist studying the mental and neural representation of numbers. I study the representation of numbers like "7" and of arithmetic operations. In addition to symbolic representations of number, we also have an intuitive feeling of number that we get when we look at a set of objects but do not have time to count them; I also study this "number sense" and the neural and psychological mechanisms by which it is generated.


I'm currently a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Pennsylvania in Liz Brannon's lab. I got my PhD in neurobiology from Duke University under Michael Platt and Liz Brannon.
